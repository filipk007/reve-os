# Hologram

## Company
- **Domain:** hologram.io
- **Industry:** IoT Connectivity Platform
- **Size:** ~200 employees
- **Stage:** Series B+

## What They Sell
Global IoT cellular connectivity platform. eUICC-enabled Hyper SIM cards + connectivity management dashboard. 550+ carrier partnerships across 190+ countries with automatic failover. Up to 300Mbps speeds, 50ms latency, 99.95% uptime SLA.

## Target ICP
- **Sweet spot:** SMB companies (6-200 employees) that build or deploy hardware with cellular connectivity
- **Typical customer:** Builds GPS trackers, IoT sensors, e-scooters, agricultural devices, industrial monitors, or medical devices
- **Deploys:** 50 to 10,000+ cellular-connected devices in the field
- **Needs:** Multi-carrier SIM management, global coverage, low data plans (5MB-1GB/month/device)
- **Company size:** 80%+ of closed-won are SMB (under 200 employees), with some mid-market and rare enterprise

## Qualification Criteria
Company's CORE BUSINESS must involve building or deploying hardware that needs cellular SIM management at scale.

Must-haves:
- Manufactures, assembles, or deploys physical hardware devices
- Devices use or could use cellular connectivity (LTE, LTE-M, CAT-M1, NB-IoT, 2G/3G, 4G/5G)
- Manages a fleet of 50+ devices deployed in the field (not lab/office)

Disqualifiers:
- Pure software/SaaS companies with no hardware component
- Companies that only WRITE ABOUT IoT (blogs, research, sustainability reports) but don't build devices
- Chip/component manufacturers (AMD, Qualcomm) — sell TO device makers, wrong value chain position
- Carriers, MVNOs, or connectivity competitors
- Hard-exclusion industries: fashion/retail, banking, insurance, food CPG, HR SaaS, media, legal, PE/VC, hospitality

## Competitive Landscape
- **Twilio (Super SIM):** Largest competitor. API-first, developer-focused. Strong brand but higher prices.
- **1NCE:** Flat-rate IoT connectivity. Aggressive pricing, limited flexibility.
- **KORE Wireless:** Enterprise-focused managed IoT connectivity. Less self-service.
- **Soracom:** Developer-friendly, strong in Japan/Asia. Smaller global footprint.
- **Aeris:** Legacy M2M provider. Several CW customers switched FROM Aeris.
- **Carrier-direct (AT&T IoT, T-Mobile IoT, Verizon M2M):** Limited to single carrier, no failover. Several CW customers switched FROM carrier-direct plans.

Hologram's edge: Multi-carrier failover (no single point of failure), eUICC remote SIM management, self-service dashboard, developer-friendly API, global coverage without managing multiple carrier contracts.

## Personas
### Founder / CEO
- **Why they buy:** Building a connected product, need reliable global connectivity without managing multiple carrier contracts
- **What they care about:** Cost per device, coverage reliability, speed to deploy
- **Opening angle:** "Scaling your device fleet globally without carrier headaches"

### CTO / VP Engineering
- **Why they buy:** Need self-service API for SIM management, carrier failover, device fleet monitoring
- **What they care about:** API quality, developer experience, multi-carrier redundancy
- **Opening angle:** "One API to manage SIMs across 550+ carriers"

### Embedded / Firmware Engineer
- **Why they buy:** Evaluating connectivity options during hardware development
- **What they care about:** Module compatibility, AT commands, coverage maps, test SIMs
- **Opening angle:** "Test SIM that works out of the box with your dev board"

### VP Operations / Director of Operations
- **Why they buy:** Managing fleet connectivity costs, dealing with outages, carrier management
- **What they care about:** Uptime SLA, dashboard visibility, bulk SIM management, cost optimization
- **Opening angle:** "Real-time dashboard for your entire device fleet"
