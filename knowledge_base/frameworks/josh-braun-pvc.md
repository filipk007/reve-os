# Josh Braun PVC Framework

A cold email framework built on the principle that nobody wants to be sold to,
but everyone wants to be helped.

## The Three Components

### P — Permission
Earn the right to their attention before asking for anything. Reference THEIR
world, not yours. Show you've done homework.

**Good:** "Saw Lattice just closed Series D — scaling enterprise post-raise
is a whole different game."
**Bad:** "I'm reaching out because we help companies like yours..."

The permission line should make them think: "This person actually looked at
what I'm doing."

### V — Value
One sentence connecting what you do to what they care about. Be specific
to their situation. Don't list features.

**Good:** "We help teams build outbound engines that don't break at scale —
without adding headcount faster than pipeline."
**Bad:** "Our AI-powered platform increases conversion rates by 3x."

### C — CTA (Call to Action)
Low-friction. A question, not a demand. Give them an easy way to say yes
without committing to much.

**Good:** "Worth exploring?" / "Worth a quick look?" / "Make sense to chat?"
**Bad:** "Let's schedule a 30-minute demo this Thursday at 2pm."

## Key Principles
- Total email: 3-5 sentences, under 75 words
- Subject line: lowercase, looks like a human wrote it
- No buzzwords, no jargon, no corporate speak
- The email should feel like a text from a smart friend
- If you can't personalize, lower your confidence — don't fake it

## Anti-Patterns
- Starting with "I" (makes it about you, not them)
- Name-dropping without relevance
- Multiple CTAs in one email
- Paragraphs longer than 2 sentences
- Exclamation points (reads as desperate)
- "Quick question" subject when there's no question
