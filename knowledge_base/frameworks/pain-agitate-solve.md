# Pain-Agitate-Solve (PAS) Framework

Lead with a specific pain the prospect likely has. Agitate the cost of
inaction. Present your solution as the natural next step.

PAS works because it mirrors how people actually make decisions: they
don't buy until the status quo becomes uncomfortable enough to change.

## When to Use PAS

- You have strong signal data about their specific pain
- The prospect likely knows they have the problem but hasn't prioritized it
- Short-form content: cold emails, LinkedIn DMs, ad copy
- The pain is concrete and measurable (not abstract)

## When NOT to Use PAS

- You're guessing at their pain (if you're wrong, you lose credibility)
- The prospect doesn't know they have a problem (use BAB instead)
- Longer-form content where you need more narrative arc (use AIDA)
- The pain is sensitive or personal (agitation feels aggressive)

## The Three Components

### P — Pain

State a specific pain the prospect likely experiences. The more specific,
the more it resonates. Generic pain = generic response (none).

**Rules:**
- Must be based on real signal data, not assumptions
- Specificity beats breadth — name the exact pain, not the category
- Use their language, not yours
- Frame it as an observation, not an accusation

**Good:** "Scaling from 3 to 10 AEs usually means outbound quality
tanks right when pipeline matters most."

**Bad:** "Many companies struggle with outbound." (too vague, everyone
ignores this)

**Good:** "Hiring SDRs faster than you can ramp them usually creates
more pipeline mess than pipeline."

**Bad:** "Is your sales team underperforming?" (accusatory, triggers
defensiveness)

### A — Agitate

Make the cost of inaction concrete. Don't be aggressive — be empathetic.
You're helping them see what they already feel but haven't quantified.

**Rules:**
- Agitate the COST, not the emotion
- Be empathetic, not fear-mongering
- Quantify when possible (time, money, opportunity cost)
- One agitation point, not a list of five

**Good:** "Every week that outbound stays manual, your AEs spend 30%
of their time on work that doesn't close deals."

**Bad:** "If you don't fix this NOW, your competitors will eat you
alive!!!" (fear-mongering, unprofessional)

**Good:** "That's pipeline you're leaving on the table while your
team burns cycles on the wrong accounts."

**Bad:** "This is a massive problem that's destroying your business."
(hyperbolic, loses trust)

### S — Solve

Present your solution as the natural resolution. Don't list features.
Connect to the specific pain and agitation you just described.

**Rules:**
- The solution should feel like a relief, not a sales pitch
- One sentence max — you're opening a door, not delivering a demo
- Connect directly to the pain you stated
- End with a low-friction CTA

**Good:** "We help teams build outbound engines that scale with the
team — not against it. Worth a quick look?"

**Bad:** "Our AI-powered platform features 47 integrations, real-time
analytics, and machine learning algorithms that..." (feature dump)

## Full PAS Example (Cold Email)

> Subject: outbound at 10 AEs
>
> Scaling from 3 to 10 AEs usually means outbound quality tanks right
> when pipeline matters most.
>
> Every week it stays manual, your best reps spend a third of their
> time on research instead of selling.
>
> We help teams like [company] build outbound that scales with the
> team, not against it. Worth a quick look?

**Word count:** 58. Under 75. Every word earns its place.

## PAS vs PVC

PAS and PVC both work for cold outbound. The difference:

| | PAS | PVC |
|---|-----|-----|
| Opens with | Their pain | Your homework (permission) |
| Emotional lever | Cost of inaction | Relevance + earned attention |
| Best when | Pain is obvious and specific | Pain needs context |
| Risk | Wrong pain = instant delete | Less risky, more flexible |

Use PAS when you have high-confidence signal data about their pain.
Use PVC when personalization is your strongest card.

## Anti-Patterns
- Agitating a pain they don't have (you guessed wrong)
- Being aggressive instead of empathetic in the agitation
- Listing features in the solve section
- Making the agitation longer than the pain (feels manipulative)
- Using PAS for problems the prospect doesn't know about yet
