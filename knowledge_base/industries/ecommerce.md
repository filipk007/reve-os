# E-commerce Industry Context

## Market Dynamics

E-commerce is fast-moving, metrics-obsessed, and intensely competitive.
Buyers evaluate tools based on measurable ROI, often within 30 days.
If you can't show impact on revenue, conversion, or efficiency quickly,
you'll be replaced.

### D2C vs Marketplace Dynamics

**Direct-to-Consumer (D2C):**
- Full control over customer experience and data
- Higher customer acquisition costs (CAC)
- Brand building is everything
- Margins are thin — efficiency matters
- Tech stack is their competitive advantage

**Marketplace Sellers (Amazon, Shopify):**
- Platform rules dictate strategy
- Less control over customer data
- Advertising costs are the biggest lever
- Logistics and fulfillment are key differentiators
- Margin pressure from platform fees (15-45%)

## Buyer Personas

- **CMO/VP Marketing:** Cares about CAC, ROAS, brand awareness,
  attribution, creative performance. Often the primary buyer for
  martech tools.
- **VP E-commerce/Digital:** Cares about conversion rate, AOV, site
  experience, cart abandonment, mobile optimization
- **Head of Growth:** Cares about channel diversification, LTV/CAC
  ratio, retention, experimentation velocity
- **VP Operations/Supply Chain:** Cares about fulfillment speed,
  inventory accuracy, logistics cost, returns management
- **CFO:** Cares about unit economics, margin expansion, cash flow
  cycle, marketing efficiency ratio (MER)
- **CTO:** Cares about platform stability, page speed, API
  integrations, headless architecture, data infrastructure

## Key Metrics (Know These)

| Metric | What It Means | Good Benchmark |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| AOV | Average Order Value | Varies by vertical ($50-200 typical) |
| LTV | Customer Lifetime Value | 3x+ CAC for healthy unit economics |
| CAC | Customer Acquisition Cost | Trending up across all channels |
| ROAS | Return on Ad Spend | 3-5x for paid social, 5-10x for search |
| CVR | Conversion Rate | 2-4% for D2C, higher for repeat purchasers |
| MER | Marketing Efficiency Ratio | Total revenue / total marketing spend |
| Cart Abandonment | % who add to cart but don't buy | 65-75% is industry average |
| Repeat Purchase Rate | % of customers who buy again | 25-40% is healthy |

**Messaging Implication:** Speak in metrics. "We increase conversion
rate by 15%" beats "We improve the shopping experience."

## Seasonal Buying Patterns

E-commerce is intensely seasonal. Timing your outreach matters.

| Period | What's Happening | GTM Implication |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Jan-Feb | Post-holiday analysis, budget planning | Pitch ROI tools, analytics |
| Mar-Apr | Q2 planning, new channel testing | Pitch growth tools |
| May-Jun | Pre-BFCM preparation starts | Pitch anything BFCM-ready |
| Jul-Aug | BFCM strategy locked, Prime Day | Hard to sell new tools |
| Sep-Oct | Tech freeze for BFCM | Don't pitch — they won't switch |
| Nov-Dec | BFCM, holiday peak | Don't pitch — they're executing |

**Critical Rule:** Never try to sell a new tool between September and
December. They're frozen. Reach out in January with BFCM retrospective
insights.

## Common Martech Stack

- **Platform:** Shopify Plus, BigCommerce, Magento, Salesforce Commerce
- **Email/SMS:** Klaviyo, Attentive, Postscript, Braze
- **Analytics:** Google Analytics 4, Triple Whale, Northbeam, Polar
- **Advertising:** Meta Ads, Google Ads, TikTok, Pinterest
- **Reviews:** Yotpo, Stamped, Okendo, Bazaarvoice
- **Subscriptions:** Recharge, Skio, Ordergroove
- **Loyalty:** Smile.io, LoyaltyLion, Yotpo
- **CMS/Content:** Contentful, Sanity (headless), Shogun
- **Personalization:** Nosto, Dynamic Yield, Rebuy
- **CDP:** Segment, mParticle, Treasure Data

## Industry-Specific Pain Points
- Rising CAC across all paid channels (especially Meta post-ATT)
- Attribution fragmentation (iOS privacy, cookie deprecation)
- Margin pressure from shipping costs and returns
- Inventory management and demand forecasting
- Standing out in a crowded D2C market
- Balancing growth with profitability (the "profitable growth" era)
- Platform dependency risk (Shopify, Amazon policy changes)
- Content production volume needed for paid social

## Current Trends (2025-2026)
- AI-generated product content and creative at scale
- First-party data strategies replacing third-party cookies
- Headless commerce and composable architecture
- Social commerce maturation (TikTok Shop, Instagram)
- Subscription model evolution (flexible vs rigid)
- Same-day/next-day delivery expectations expanding
- Sustainability and circular commerce demands
- Retail media networks as a new revenue stream

## Language That Resonates
- "Measurable ROI within 30 days"
- "Increase AOV without increasing ad spend"
- "Own your customer data"
- "Works with your existing Shopify/Klaviyo stack"
- "Built for BFCM-scale traffic"

## Language to Avoid
- "Long-term strategic investment" (they want results now)
- "6-month implementation" (deal killer)
- "Enterprise-grade" to SMB merchants (sounds expensive and slow)
- "Omnichannel" without specifics (overused, vague)

## Signals Worth Referencing
- New product launches or category expansion
- Funding rounds (especially Series A-B for D2C brands)
- New channel launches (TikTok Shop, Amazon)
- Platform migrations (Shopify Plus upgrade, going headless)
- Seasonal hiring spikes (signals growth)
