# FinTech Industry Context

## Regulatory Environment

FinTech operates under heavy regulatory scrutiny. Every GTM message must
acknowledge this reality — buyers won't trust you if you ignore it.

**Key Compliance Standards:**
- **SOC 2 Type II:** Table stakes for any B2B fintech vendor
- **PCI DSS:** Required if handling payment card data
- **SOX Compliance:** Relevant for publicly traded fintech companies
- **AML/KYC:** Anti-money laundering and know-your-customer requirements
- **GDPR/CCPA:** Data privacy regulations affecting customer data handling
- **State-by-state licensing:** Money transmitter licenses vary by state

**Messaging Implication:** Lead with compliance readiness. "We're SOC 2
certified" opens more doors than any feature claim.

## Buyer Personas

- **CTO/VP Engineering:** Cares about API reliability, uptime SLAs,
  security architecture, developer experience, integration complexity
- **CFO/VP Finance:** Cares about total cost of ownership, audit trail,
  regulatory risk, ROI timeline
- **CISO/Head of Security:** Cares about data residency, encryption,
  access controls, penetration testing, vendor risk assessment
- **Head of Product:** Cares about time-to-market, API flexibility,
  white-label capabilities, end-user experience
- **Chief Compliance Officer:** Cares about audit readiness, reporting
  capabilities, regulatory change management

## Common Tech Stacks

- **Banking infrastructure:** Plaid, Stripe, Marqeta, Galileo, Synapse
- **Compliance:** Alloy, Jumio, Persona, ComplyAdvantage
- **Data/Analytics:** Snowflake, dbt, Looker, Segment
- **Core banking:** Thought Machine, Mambu, Temenos, FIS
- **Communication:** Twilio, SendGrid, Braze

## Buying Cycle

- **Length:** 3-9 months (longer for regulated products)
- **Stakeholders:** 5-8 people typical, including compliance and legal
- **Procurement quirks:** Vendor security questionnaires (VSQs) are standard,
  expect 50-200 question security reviews before closing
- **Budget holders:** Engineering budget for dev tools, Finance budget for
  infrastructure, Compliance budget for regulatory tools
- **Decision bottleneck:** Security and compliance review — start this
  early, not after the champion is sold

## Industry-Specific Pain Points

- Regulatory complexity slowing product launches
- Fraud prevention without destroying user experience
- Legacy system integration (especially with traditional banks)
- Scaling compliance as you enter new markets
- Balancing security with speed-to-market
- Rising customer acquisition costs in a crowded market
- Banking-as-a-service partner reliability and concentration risk

## Current Trends (2025-2026)

- Embedded finance going mainstream (every company becomes a fintech)
- AI-driven fraud detection and compliance automation
- Real-time payments infrastructure (FedNow adoption)
- Open banking APIs and data portability
- Crypto/blockchain regulation maturing
- Banking-as-a-service consolidation after partner bank failures
- Vertical-specific fintech (healthcare payments, construction lending)

## Language That Resonates
- "Compliance-ready from day one"
- "Built for regulated environments"
- "Security isn't a feature — it's the foundation"
- "Ship faster without compliance bottlenecks"
- "Enterprise-grade reliability, startup-speed deployment"

## Language to Avoid
- "Move fast and break things" (terrifies regulated companies)
- "Disrupting banking" (incumbents are your partners and customers)
- "AI-powered" without specifics (triggers compliance skepticism)
- "We handle compliance for you" (no one believes this)

## Signals Worth Referencing
- New state licensing or market expansion
- Funding rounds (Series B+ = scaling = new vendor needs)
- Compliance team hiring (growing regulatory surface area)
- Banking partner changes (risk or opportunity)
- Product launches requiring new infrastructure
- Executive hires from traditional finance (signals maturation)
