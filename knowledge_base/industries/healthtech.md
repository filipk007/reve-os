# HealthTech Industry Context

## Regulatory Environment

Healthcare is the most regulated industry in tech. HIPAA isn't optional,
and buyers will disqualify vendors who don't understand the compliance
landscape before the first demo.

**Key Compliance Standards:**
- **HIPAA:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act — governs
  protected health information (PHI). BAA (Business Associate Agreement)
  required for any vendor handling PHI.
- **HITECH Act:** Extends HIPAA to business associates, increases penalties
- **SOC 2 Type II:** Expected for any SaaS vendor selling to health systems
- **HITRUST CSF:** Gold standard for healthcare security certification,
  increasingly required by large health systems
- **FDA clearance:** Required for clinical decision support and diagnostic
  tools (Class I, II, or III depending on risk)
- **21 CFR Part 11:** Electronic records and signatures in clinical contexts
- **State privacy laws:** Vary by state, some stricter than HIPAA

**Messaging Implication:** Always lead with compliance posture. A signed
BAA is the price of admission, not a differentiator.

## Buyer Personas

### Clinical Buyers
- **CMIO (Chief Medical Informatics Officer):** Cares about clinical
  workflow impact, evidence-based outcomes, physician adoption
- **CNO (Chief Nursing Officer):** Cares about nursing workflow, alert
  fatigue, documentation burden
- **Department Chiefs:** Cares about specialty-specific needs, clinical
  outcomes, staff satisfaction

### Administrative Buyers
- **CIO/CTO:** Cares about integration, security, total cost, IT
  resource requirements, EHR compatibility
- **CFO:** Cares about ROI, reimbursement impact, implementation cost,
  time-to-value
- **VP Operations:** Cares about efficiency gains, staff utilization,
  patient throughput
- **CISO:** Cares about data security, access controls, incident response

### Decision Dynamic
Clinical buyers and administrative buyers often have competing priorities.
Clinical wants the best tool for patient outcomes. Administrative wants
the most cost-effective, easiest to implement. You need both.

## EHR Integration

**The EHR is the center of gravity.** Any healthtech product must integrate
with the major EHR systems or it won't be considered.

**Major EHR Systems:**
- **Epic:** ~38% market share. FHIR APIs, App Orchard marketplace.
  Epic integration is table stakes for large health systems.
- **Oracle Health (Cerner):** ~25% market share. Open APIs, Oracle
  cloud migration underway.
- **MEDITECH:** Strong in community hospitals
- **athenahealth:** Strong in ambulatory/outpatient

**Integration Reality:** EHR integration takes 3-12 months. Budget for
it in your sales cycle and pricing. "We integrate with Epic" must mean
you've actually done it, with references.

## Sales Cycle

- **Length:** 6-18 months (can extend to 24 for large health systems)
- **Stakeholders:** 8-15+ people (clinical champions, IT, compliance,
  procurement, C-suite, sometimes board approval)
- **Procurement:** Formal RFP process common for deals >$100K
- **Pilot requirements:** Most health systems require a pilot period
  (60-180 days) before full commitment
- **Budget cycles:** Annual, typically set Q3-Q4 for next fiscal year.
  If you miss the budget cycle, you wait 12 months.
- **Decision bottlenecks:** Compliance review, IT security assessment,
  clinical validation committee

## Industry-Specific Pain Points
- Clinician burnout and documentation burden
- EHR usability and interoperability
- Revenue cycle management inefficiency
- Patient acquisition cost rising (especially D2C health)
- Staffing shortages across clinical roles
- Data silos between departments and systems
- Prior authorization burden and denials
- Telehealth integration and reimbursement uncertainty

## Current Trends (2025-2026)
- AI-assisted clinical documentation (ambient listening)
- Value-based care models shifting reimbursement incentives
- Remote patient monitoring and hospital-at-home
- Mental health and behavioral health tech expansion
- Healthcare AI regulation (FDA framework evolving)
- Payer-provider convergence
- Health equity and social determinants of health (SDOH) data

## Language That Resonates
- "Built for clinical workflows, not against them"
- "HIPAA-compliant by design, not by afterthought"
- "Reduces documentation burden by X minutes per encounter"
- "Integrates with your EHR — not around it"
- "Evidence-based, clinically validated"

## Language to Avoid
- "Disrupting healthcare" (clinicians distrust disruption)
- "AI-powered" without clinical evidence (triggers skepticism)
- "Replace your current system" (terrifies IT and clinical staff)
- "Easy to implement" (nothing in healthcare is easy to implement)
- "Move fast" (healthcare moves deliberately for good reason)

## Signals Worth Referencing
- New CIO/CTO/CMIO hire (signals technology strategy shift)
- EHR migration or upgrade (window for new vendor evaluation)
- Health system merger or acquisition (consolidation = new tooling)
- Value-based care contract announcements
- Clinical trial launches or research partnerships
- HITRUST certification achievement (signals security maturity)
