# Media & Entertainment Industry Context

## Common Pain Points
- Massive video archives (10,000+ hours) with no searchable index
- Manual tagging and metadata entry is expensive and doesn't scale
- Content discovery takes hours — editors scrubbing through footage
- Monetization pressure — need to repurpose content faster (clips, highlights, social)
- Compliance and content moderation at scale (DMCA, brand safety)
- Multiple platforms = multiple formats = content fragmentation

## Buyer Personas
- **VP Content Operations:** Cares about workflow speed, asset discovery, team productivity
- **Head of Post-Production:** Cares about editing efficiency, clip discovery, rough cut automation
- **Director of Digital / Social:** Cares about clip generation speed, trending moment capture
- **CTO / VP Engineering (media tech):** Cares about API integrations, MAM compatibility, scalability

## Language That Resonates
- "Find the exact moment in 50,000 hours of footage"
- "Your archive is a goldmine — if you can actually search it"
- "From raw footage to social clip in minutes, not hours"
- "Stop paying people to watch video. Let AI watch it for you."

## Language to Avoid
- "Disrupt" (media people hate this word)
- "Content is king" (cliche, everyone says it)
- "AI-generated content" (they want AI for understanding, not replacement)

## Signals Worth Referencing
- Streaming platform launches or expansions
- Hiring for content ops, post-production, or media engineering roles
- Partnerships with MAM providers (Iconik, Frame.io, Mimir)
- Rights deals or content library acquisitions
- Social media strategy shifts (more short-form = more clipping need)
