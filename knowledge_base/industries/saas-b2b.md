# SaaS B2B Industry Context

## Common Pain Points
- Pipeline generation at scale without proportional headcount growth
- CRM hygiene and data quality across the GTM stack
- SDR productivity and ramp time
- Marketing-to-sales handoff friction
- Tech stack bloat (too many tools, not enough integration)
- Proving ROI on GTM investments to the board

## Buyer Personas (by title)
- **VP Sales:** Cares about quota attainment, rep productivity, forecast accuracy
- **VP Marketing:** Cares about pipeline contribution, CAC, attribution
- **RevOps / GTM Ops:** Cares about data quality, workflow automation, tool consolidation
- **CRO:** Cares about revenue efficiency, GTM alignment, board metrics
- **CEO (Series A-C):** Cares about capital efficiency, growth rate, team scaling

## Language That Resonates
- "Pipeline that's repeatable, not random"
- "Outbound without the headcount bloat"
- "Systems that scale with you, not against you"
- "From founder-led sales to a real GTM engine"

## Language to Avoid
- "AI-powered" (overused, triggers skepticism)
- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" (every startup says this)
- "End-to-end" (vague, means nothing)
- "Best-in-class" (prove it, don't claim it)

## Signals Worth Referencing
- Funding rounds (especially Series B-D — scaling stage)
- Executive hires (new VP Sales = new playbook = openness to tools)
- Tech adoption signals (switching CRMs, adding enrichment tools)
- Job postings (hiring SDRs = outbound investment)
- Product launches or market expansion
