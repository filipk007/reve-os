# Security & Surveillance Industry Context

## Common Pain Points
- Thousands of camera feeds, not enough analysts to watch them
- Incident review takes hours of manual footage scrubbing
- False positive fatigue from basic motion detection systems
- Compliance requirements (data retention, audit trails, privacy)
- Existing systems can detect motion but can't understand context
- Integration with legacy VMS (Video Management Systems)

## Buyer Personas
- **Director of Security Operations:** Cares about threat detection accuracy, response time, alert quality
- **VP of Physical Security:** Cares about coverage, cost reduction, compliance
- **CTO (security tech company):** Cares about API capability, model accuracy, edge deployment
- **Head of Smart City / Municipal IT:** Cares about traffic management, public safety, scalability

## Language That Resonates
- "Know what happened without watching 1,000 hours of footage"
- "Context-aware detection — not just motion, but meaning"
- "From reactive monitoring to proactive intelligence"
- "Search your camera feeds like you search the internet"

## Language to Avoid
- "Surveillance" in a negative connotation (they prefer "monitoring" or "safety")
- "Replace security guards" (sensitive topic, focus on augmentation)
- Anything that sounds like mass surveillance without privacy consideration

## Signals Worth Referencing
- Expanding camera infrastructure (new facilities, campuses)
- Security incidents that made news (theft, trespassing at scale)
- Regulatory changes around video retention or monitoring
- Smart city initiatives or municipal contracts
- Hiring for security operations or intelligence analysts
