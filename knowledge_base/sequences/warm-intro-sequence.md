# Warm Intro Sequence

Follow-up sequence for warm introductions and referrals. Shorter than cold
sequences because the trust bridge already exists. Your job is to honor
the introduction and convert warmth into a conversation.

## Why Warm Intros Are Different

- The referrer's reputation is on the line — respect that
- The prospect already has context — don't over-explain
- Trust is borrowed, not earned — you need to earn it quickly
- Shorter cycle: 3 touches over 7 days, not 5 over 21

## Sequence Overview

| Step | Day | Purpose | Tone |
|------|-----|---------|------|
| 1 | 0 | Post-intro email | Warm, specific, concise |
| 2 | 3 | Value-add follow-up | Helpful, generous |
| 3 | 7 | Gentle close | Respectful, open-door |

## Step 1: Post-Intro Email (Same Day)

**Purpose:** Respond within hours. Speed signals respect for the referrer.
Reply-all to thank the referrer, then follow up directly with the prospect.
**Subject:** Keep the intro thread, or "[Referrer] suggested we connect"
**Tone:** Warm, grateful, concise. Friend-of-a-friend, not cold caller.

**Reply-All:** "Thanks for connecting us, [referrer]! I'll take it from here."

**Direct Follow-Up:**
> Hey [name] — [referrer] mentioned you're [specific context].
>
> We've been helping similar teams [specific value]. Thought it might
> be worth a quick conversation. Would 15 minutes this week work?

**Do:** Respond same day (within 2 hrs), reference referrer's context,
under 5 sentences, propose specific time frame.
**Don't:** Wait 24+ hours, send long company overview, forget to thank
the referrer, use generic templates.

## Step 2: Value-Add Follow-Up (Day 3)

**Purpose:** If they didn't respond to Step 1, follow up with something
genuinely useful. Don't just "bump" the email — add value.

**Structure:**
- Brief reference to intro
- Share a relevant resource, insight, or benchmark
- Restate CTA lightly

**Subject Line Pattern:** Re: original thread (keep the chain)

**Tone:** Helpful, not pushy. You're sharing something useful regardless
of whether they take the meeting.

**Example:**
> Hey [name] — wanted to share something that might be relevant
> regardless of whether we connect.
>
> [Relevant resource — benchmark, case study, insight related to
> their specific situation]
>
> If [referrer]'s instinct was right that this could be useful for
> [their company], happy to spend 15 minutes walking through what
> we're seeing. If not, hope the resource helps either way.

## Step 3: Gentle Close (Day 7)

**Purpose:** Close the loop respectfully. Don't guilt-trip. Don't
reference the referrer as leverage. Leave the door open.

**Structure:**
- Acknowledge their busy schedule
- One final clear CTA
- Open door for future

**Subject Line Pattern:** Re: original thread

**Tone:** Respectful, zero pressure. This is about them, not you.

**Example:**
> Hey [name] — totally understand if the timing's off. Know how
> it goes.
>
> If this is something worth revisiting down the road, I'm easy
> to find. Either way, appreciate [referrer] making the connection.
>
> All the best with [specific thing they're working on].

## Rules for the Full Sequence
- ALWAYS respond to the intro same day
- Three touches max — after that, move to a long-term nurture list
- Never guilt-trip using the referrer's name ("I promised [referrer]
  I'd follow up...")
- If the referrer asks how it went, give them an honest, brief update
- Thank the referrer regardless of outcome — they're more likely to
  refer again
- Track: response rate by referrer source, conversion rate, time to
  first meeting

## Warm vs Cold Comparison

| Metric | Cold | Warm |
|--------|------|------|
| Avg reply rate | 5-12% | 25-40% |
| Touches needed | 5 | 3 |
| Time to meeting | 14-21 days | 3-7 days |
| Sequence duration | 21 days | 7 days |
