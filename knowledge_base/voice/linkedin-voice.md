# LinkedIn Voice Guide

LinkedIn is not email. It's a different channel with different norms,
different attention spans, and different expectations. Content that works
in email often falls flat on LinkedIn, and vice versa.

## Core Principle

You're a colleague having a conversation — not a sales rep running a
sequence. Everything should feel like it came from someone who works in
the industry and has a genuine take.

## Connection Notes

**Character Limit:** 300 characters. Every word must earn its place.

**Do:**
- Reference something specific (their content, a mutual connection,
  a company milestone)
- Keep it to 1-2 sentences
- Sound like a human, not a template
- Give a genuine reason for connecting

**Don't:**
- Pitch your product
- Mention your company name or title
- Use generic templates ("I'd love to connect!")
- Include a CTA or meeting request
- Use "I came across your profile" (everyone says this)

**Good:** "Your take on PLG vs sales-led was spot on — especially the
bit about founder-led sales being a trap. Would love to connect."

**Bad:** "Hi! I'm the VP of Sales at AcmeCorp. We help companies like
yours increase pipeline by 3x. Let's connect!"

## DMs (Direct Messages)

**Rules:**
- First DM is never a pitch
- Keep it under 4 sentences
- Reference something real (their post, shared context, mutual connection)
- Ask a question or share a resource — give before you ask
- No attachments or links in the first DM
- No voice notes unless you have an established relationship

**Tone:** Colleague, not salesperson. Curious, not pitchy.

**Good:** "Your post about the SDR model breaking at scale — we're
seeing the same thing. Curious what you're experimenting with instead?"

**Bad:** "Hi [name], hope you're doing well! I wanted to reach out
because our AI-powered platform helps teams like yours..." (instant
block)

## Content Comments

Comments are the highest-ROI LinkedIn activity for outbound. A good
comment gets you on a prospect's radar without any pitch.

**Do:** Read the full post first, add a unique perspective or data point,
ask a genuine follow-up question, keep to 2-4 sentences.
**Don't:** "Great post!" (zero-value), pitch in comments, copy-paste
formats, comment on every post, tag your company or link to your product.

**Good:** "This matches what we've seen — the teams separating
personalization from research as distinct workflows scale 2-3x
faster. Have you found a breakpoint where manual research stops
being viable?"

**Bad:** "Great insights! At AcmeCorp, we've solved this exact
problem with our AI platform. Check us out at acme.com!"

## Content Posts (If Applicable)

If the client or skill generates LinkedIn post content:

**Structure:**
- Hook line (stop the scroll — first line is everything)
- White space between lines (dense paragraphs don't get read)
- One core idea per post
- End with a question or take that invites discussion

**Rules:**
- Max 1-2 hashtags (more looks spammy)
- No emoji overload (1-2 max, or none)
- No "Agree?" as a CTA (lazy engagement bait)
- No "I'm humbled to announce" (performative)
- Short sentences, lots of line breaks
- Be opinionated — lukewarm takes get no engagement

## Tone Calibration

| Context | Tone | Length |
|---------|------|--------|
| Connection note | Peer, specific | 1-2 sentences (300 chars) |
| First DM | Curious, generous | 2-4 sentences |
| Follow-up DM | Warm, direct | 2-3 sentences |
| Comment | Substantive, conversational | 2-4 sentences |
| Post | Opinionated, clear | 5-15 lines with whitespace |

## LinkedIn vs Email Voice Differences

| | LinkedIn | Email |
|---|---------|-------|
| Length | Shorter | Slightly longer OK |
| Tone | More casual, more opinionated | Professional-casual |
| CTA | Softer, question-based | Can be more direct |
| Personalization | Profile-based, content-based | Signal-based, company-based |
| Formality | Colleague at a conference | Colleague sending a note |
| Follow-up cadence | Lighter touch, more spaced | Structured sequence |

## Anti-Patterns
- Obvious automation (generic comments, mass DMs)
- Treating LinkedIn like email (long pitches in DMs)
- Hashtag spam (#sales #marketing #AI #growth #leadership)
- "Congrats on the new role!" followed immediately by a pitch
- Connecting and immediately pitching in the welcome DM
