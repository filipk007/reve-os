Summary
Landon Schlag of CRM Magnetics met with Laura Fredericks to discuss overhauling their messy HubSpot system, which currently suffers from poor data quality, a messy sales side with no automation or proper properties, and an inability to forecast despite their long, high-value enterprise sales cycles. Laura Fredericks outlined a desired future state for reporting, focusing on pipeline velocity for sales meetings and a leadership dashboard integrating data from investor relations, direct sales, and channel partnerships, to eliminate the need for spreadsheets. Landon Schlag introduced CRM Magnetics' four-layer framework (Foundation, Automation, Visibility, and Optimization), emphasizing the critical need for automation to manage inconsistent lead follow-up and ensure clean life cycle stage progression, and outlined flexible RevOps as a service monthly tiers for the engagement, scheduling a technical scoping and proposal call as the next step, where Laura Fredericks will invite Ben Morin and other stakeholders.


Details
* Introduction and Context Landon Schlag of CRM Magnetics, a full-service CRM optimization agency specializing in HubSpot, met with Laura Fredericks, who has been in their Chief Marketing Officer role for four years and also oversees sales. Laura Fredericks is inheriting a messy HubSpot system that was not set up by them, which is a common scenario for CRM Magnetics clients (00:00:00). The goal of the conversation was to discuss strategy, identify where HubSpot is breaking down, and map out a plan for system cleanup (00:01:01).
* Key Pain Points and HubSpot Usage Laura Fredericks summarized their main pain points as a messy sales side, data quality issues, and the need for a simple standard operating procedure with dashboards that have "minimal fluff" (00:01:42). The company uses HubSpot for investor relations and sales, with a future goal of using it for channel partner sales. The investor relations side is currently clean and has a pipeline and dashboard in development, but the sales side suffers from improperly set up properties, lack of lead scoring, no discovery questions, and a total absence of sequences or automations. Users are only utilizing the search box to find contacts and review the latest notes, making forecasting nearly impossible (00:02:44).
* Company Profile and Sales Context Laura Fredericks described their business as having large enterprise sales with long, consultative sales cycles, and each license is a minimum of two million (00:03:37). Landon Schlag expressed surprise that the HubSpot system was not cleaned up sooner given the high value of the deals and the potential for improper reporting and forecasting. Laura Fredericks explained that HubSpot was temporarily unused between April and October of the previous year, with the team relying on a spreadsheet, which led to the clean-up falling by the wayside until now, when it is becoming necessary (00:04:19). The sales team is currently a "hodge podge" of a sales admin, a sales engineer coming on board, various executives selling, two business development people, and one full-funnel salesperson being hired, with the team growing and needing a clean system for training (00:06:10). Laura Fredericks also clarified that the company is very much a startup and just closed its first enterprise customer a week ago, which provides context for the current state of the system (00:13:32).
* Desired Future State for Reporting Laura Fredericks wants a dashboard to run sales meetings, specifically looking at pipeline velocity and the stage of larger deals, with the ability to flag discussion items. They also need the ability to report on individual salesperson's deal velocity and overall performance, with metrics tied to compensation (00:05:14). The ultimate big-picture dashboard, for the CEO or leadership, should include revenue forecasting towards the year's big number, integrating data from investor relations, direct sales, and channel partnerships (00:14:15). Landon Schlag noted that the goal of these improvements is to eliminate spreadsheets, shorten meetings, and provide instant, simplified visibility for higher-ups (00:15:17).
* CRM Magnetics' Four-Layer Framework Landon Schlag presented a four-layer systematic approach for a HubSpot overhaul: Foundation, Automation, Visibility, and Optimization. The Foundation layer involves cleaning up legacy configuration, standardizing properties, and defining a simple Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) so the system enforces a clear way of working (00:06:58) (00:09:46). Laura Fredericks noted that the qualification criteria are changing from BANT to MEDDIC, requiring different properties, and that existing properties were set up incorrectly, but life cycle stages and pipelines for direct and channel sales have been defined within HubSpot, though they are not automated (00:07:53). Landon Schlag stressed that automation of the life cycle stages is a critical and key focus point (00:08:57).
* Automation and Integration Needs Landon Schlag identified automation as crucial for saving time, reducing friction, and ensuring focus on important tasks, noting that the company's leads coming from events and trade shows are prone to falling apart without clear handoff rules. Laura Fredericks confirmed that slow lead ownership and inconsistent follow-ups are problems, but clarified the handoff issue is primarily from BD event attendees to sales, not marketing to sales (00:10:35). Laura Fredericks also has "big visions" for automation, including an automated process for scanning business cards, tagging them, and initiating sequences, as well as sequences for webinars and setting up a blog with an RSS feed (00:11:31). Additionally, Laura Fredericks flagged the intention to integrate an external tool called Dealpad with HubSpot, which updates from the CRM (00:16:17).
* Service Tiers and Next Steps Landon Schlag outlined the engagement structure, RevOps as a service model, which includes Silver, Gold, and Platinum monthly tiers with initial three-month terms (00:17:57). The Silver plan is a base of 10 hours per month at $150/hour, the Gold plan is 20 hours per month at $130/hour, and the Platinum plan is 40 hours per month at $110/hour, with the Gold plan translating to $2,600 per month (00:18:45) (00:22:00). Landon Schlag suggested Silver or Gold would be most appropriate, as the pain points are clear (00:19:35). Laura Fredericks inquired about front-loading hours, for example, 30 hours in month one and then tapering off, which Landon Schlag confirmed is flexible as the tiers represent "base" hours (00:20:25). The next step is a technical scoping and proposal call with a solutions architect, which is scheduled for 2:30 p.m. Central on the 29th, and Laura Fredericks will invite other stakeholders, including Ben Morin (00:23:30) (00:25:26).
