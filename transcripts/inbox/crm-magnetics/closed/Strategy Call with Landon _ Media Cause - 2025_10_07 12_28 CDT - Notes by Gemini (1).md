### Details

* **Company Overview** Delaney Mullennix discussed their company's operations, noting they have shifted from traditional office leases to co-working spaces in cities like Atlanta, DC, San Francisco, and Boston due to high real estate costs and remote work trends. Landon Schlag summarized that their agency is a non-profit marketing agency focused on helping non-profits grow and expand their reach to clients ([00:00:00](#00:00:00)).

* **HubSpot Optimization and Service Offerings** Landon Schlag explained that their company is a HubSpot optimization agency offering full-service support, including implementation and training for new users, and optimization for existing users ([00:00:00](#00:00:00)). They aim to help businesses drive revenue and save time by cleaning up clunky HubSpot systems and automating tasks. Delaney Mullennix identified key areas of interest for their agency, including email sequences, engagement scoring, content scoring, and lead segmentation with appropriate messaging ([00:00:48](#00:00:48)).

* **Challenges with Current HubSpot Usage** Delaney Mullennix shared that their agency recently adopted outbound sales, which has been successful, but scaling is difficult due to their limited staff. They expressed belief that improving their HubSpot utilization could significantly enhance efficiency ([00:01:38](#00:01:38)). A primary issue with their current HubSpot setup is the inaccuracy of company and contact information, which hinders automation and makes tasks highly manual, especially for outbound sales where they must manually add new client information ([00:02:39](#00:02:39)) ([00:10:38](#00:10:38)).

* **Client Targeting and Services** Delaney Mullennix detailed their target clients as higher-level executive folks in non-profits, including executive directors, directors of fundraising, development, or marketing, depending on the organization's size and service line ([00:05:33](#00:05:33)). They noted that their agency provides digital and creative services, such as social media, paid advertising, branding, and website development, with custom scopes for each project ([00:06:39](#00:06:39)). While they prefer retainers, they often start with project work to demonstrate ROI before moving to long-term agreements ([00:05:33](#00:05:33)).

* **Deal Size and Sales Performance** Delaney Mullennix stated that their projects have a minimum of $10,000, with some retainers going up to $30,000 per month ([00:06:39](#00:06:39)). They target organizations with annual revenues of $1 million and up, with individual sales varying significantly; Delaney Mullennix mentioned closing one or two deals per month with a 40% close rate this year ([00:07:37](#00:07:37)).

* **Lead Tracking and Automation Needs** Delaney Mullennix explained that inbound leads are managed by Ellen, the Chief Revenue Officer, and Ben, with some automations for tagging visitors to consultation forms ([00:08:29](#00:08:29)). However, the manual management of these leads, even with a manageable volume of 14 per month, indicates a need for further automation to track engagement and re-engage past clients ([00:09:39](#00:09:39)). They confirmed that follow-ups and proposals are currently handled individually, though they see potential benefits in using HubSpot's sales dashboard and task lists more effectively if automation was improved ([00:12:41](#00:12:41)).

* **Data Enrichment and Accuracy** Landon Schlag emphasized the importance of clean and accurate data as a foundational element for HubSpot's effectiveness. Delaney Mullennix confirmed that their company does not use data enrichment tools like Apollo or ZoomInfo, making their data messy and often inaccurate ([00:04:45](#00:04:45)) ([00:13:48](#00:13:48)). Landon Schlag suggested Apollo as a cost-effective solution that automatically enriches contact and company data weekly, which would enable more targeted messaging based on accurate information like job titles and locations ([00:14:50](#00:14:50)).

* **Business Development Automation and Reporting** Landon Schlag proposed automating lead follow-up, scoring, and pipeline tasks to free up time for relationship building. This includes automated sequences, lead scoring based on engagement, and deal pipeline automation with task triggers for follow-ups ([00:16:48](#00:16:48)). They also highlighted the importance of sales enablement and reporting through dashboards to track pipeline stages, forecast revenue, and identify successful outreach channels, which Delaney Mullennix confirmed they have started to use ([00:17:50](#00:17:50)).

* **Customer Success and Internal Communication** Landon Schlag detailed how their services could automate ongoing engagement for customer success, including impact report reminders and renewal alerts, to ensure client retention and expansion. Delaney Mullennix noted that post-sale, new business is handed off to an account director who handles renewals, but Ellen, the Chief Growth Officer, oversees all revenue and would be interested in discussions about renewals, marketing, and both inbound and outbound strategies ([00:19:46](#00:19:46)).

* **Pricing and Process** Landon Schlag outlined their "RevOps as a Service" plan, which includes all discussed services in three tiers: Silver, Gold, and Platinum, with initial terms of three months to demonstrate immediate value ([00:22:24](#00:22:24)). The Silver plan starts at $1300 per month for 10 hours, the Gold plan at $2200 per month for 20 hours, and a platinum plan at $3600 per month for 40 hours ([00:23:14](#00:23:14)).
